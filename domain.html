Domain Names and ICANN

Here are examples of Top level domains.

ICANN stands for Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers

Domain names are essentially the name of your website. It is the human-readable form of an IP address. Its purpose is to make websites and networks easily recognizable and easily communicated rather using its ip address. Domain names can be categorized into three parts. There is the sub-domain which is the prefix or extension of the domain name which makes it easier to differentiate your website. These are the www, ftp, m, store, etc. Most websites use www but you can essentially make your own sub domain to organize it better. Then theres the domain name which is the name of the website. Lastly is the top-level domain. These are at the top of the hierarchy of the Domain Name System. Most websites use the generic top level domains which are com,org,net, and edu. You can also use country code such as us, uk ,fr, de, etc.
ICANN is arguably the top organization that manages the internet. They are responsible for allocating portions of the IP address space, managing the Domain Name System (DNS), including developing new top-level domains and managing root name servers, and assigning Internet Protocol (IP) parameters.
